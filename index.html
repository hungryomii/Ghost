Ghost is a word game in which two players take turns saying letters of the alphabet. The first player to complete a word loses. For example:
Player 1: "G"
Player 2: "H"
Player 1: "O"
Player 2: "S"
Player 1: "T"

Player 1 finishes the word "ghost" and loses the game. Players must have a word in mind that begins with the letters stated so far. For example:
Player 1: "G"
Player 2: "X" ---not valid because there is no word that begins with "GX".

Usually, only words with at least 3 or 4 characters are used. Better explanations of the game can be found here and here. This simple program, given a prefix, outputs whether each letter forces a win or loss under perfect play. The dictionary used is the European Scrabble Word List.
